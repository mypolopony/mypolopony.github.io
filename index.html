What I Do

I gather and organize diverse, often obscured data to find patterns and solve problems.

Things I've Done Recently:

- defined my own degree in Biomedical Computation at Stanford University

- spent three years working for the University of California in San Francisco in a computational neuroscience laboratory measuring brainwave activity in patients with schizophrenia

- modeled dynamic systems near and far, from protein interaction networks to seismic wave propagation to international supply chains

- spent three years in medical school at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine in New York (neurology and psychiatry)

- most recently, I've worked as a data scientist quantifying sustainability and creating intelligent algorithms to automatically identify environmentally preferable consumer and industrial goods

I am passionate about largish data, identifying obscure data sources, high-throughput information and content extraction, identity resolution, and making square pegs fit into round holes. I also have interests in dynamic systems, current events and primary sources, ontologies, transforming data, and performing analytics.
